# The Silent Epidemic: Lead Poisoning in Africa

**Live site:** [tedfoley.github.io/lead-poisoning](https://tedfoley.github.io/lead-poisoning/)

An interactive, data-driven investigation into how the failure to safely recycle lead batteries is poisoning a generation of African children.

## What it covers

1. **The Scale of the Crisis** — 800 million children globally have elevated blood lead levels; 90% of lead-related deaths occur in low- and middle-income countries
2. **Hotspots of Contamination** — Interactive Leaflet map of 12 documented sites across Africa and Asia, with detailed case narratives (Kabwe, Zamfara, Thiaroye-sur-Mer, Owino Uhuru, and more)
3. **The Battery Recycling Crisis** — How informal smelting of lead-acid batteries releases deadly lead dust into communities, and how Africa's solar boom is accelerating the problem
4. **The Cognitive Catastrophe** — IQ dose-response curves, the supra-linear relationship between lead exposure and brain damage, and the global distribution of IQ loss
5. **Economic Devastation** — Africa loses 4% of GDP to lead exposure annually; lead-related CVD kills more people than HIV, malaria, and TB combined
6. **Timeline** — Key events from 1902 (Kabwe mine opening) through 2026 (Pure Earth Audacious Project)
7. **Solutions** — Six proven interventions from formalizing recycling to transitioning to lithium-ion batteries
